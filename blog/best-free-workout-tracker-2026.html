Why Most Free Workout Trackers Aren't Really Free

The fitness app market in 2026 is crowded, but most products follow the same playbook:

  • Mandatory accounts. You can't log a single set without creating a profile. Your workout data gets stored on someone else's server — and often sold to advertisers.
  • Freemium traps. The "free" tier lets you track three exercises, then nags you to upgrade. Want to add a superset? That'll be Pro.
  • Ads everywhere. Banner ads, interstitial video ads, "recommended supplements" — between sets you're staring at marketing instead of resting.
  • Internet dependency. Many trackers are thin clients that stop working the moment you walk into a basement gym with no signal.

These pain points are real. Nobody wants to fumble with a login screen when they're ready to lift. And nobody wants their training data monetized.
