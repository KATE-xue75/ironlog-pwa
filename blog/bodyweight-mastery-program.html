Why a Progressive Bodyweight Program?

Most bodyweight programs make the same mistake: they throw you into exercises you can't do yet, or keep you stuck on the same routine forever. Bodyweight Mastery is different. It's a carefully designed 12-week progression that meets you exactly where you are — and builds a path all the way to advanced calisthenics movements.

Whether you can't do a single push-up yet, or you're chasing your first pistol squat, this program has your level. Three distinct tiers, clear progression criteria, and zero ambiguity about when to level up.

The 3-Level Progression System

The program is built around three progressive tiers. Each level has its own training split, exercise pool, volume prescription, and — critically — an objective progression gate. You don't guess when to move up; the program tells you.

🟢 Level 1 — Foundation (Weeks 1–4)

Focus: Build basic strength and proper movement patterns

Schedule: 3 days/week, full body each session

Volume: 3 sets per exercise, 8–12 reps (20–45s for holds)

Exercises: Incline pushups, knee pushups, bodyweight squats, glute bridges, dead hangs, plank holds, lunges, bird dogs

Level 1 is about earning the right to do harder things. Incline pushups let you build pushing strength at an angle you can handle. Knee pushups remove the leverage disadvantage while you build core and arm strength. Dead hangs prepare your grip and shoulder girdle for future pull-up work. Every exercise here has a purpose — nothing is filler.

Progression Gate: Complete all sets with good form → unlock Level 2.

🟠 Level 2 — Intermediate (Weeks 5–8)

Focus: Build volume and introduce harder variations

Schedule: 4 days/week — Push / Pull / Legs / Core split

Volume: 3–4 sets per exercise, 8–15 reps

Exercises: Full pushups, diamond pushups, split squats, single-leg glute bridges, negative pullups, side planks, jumping squats, pike pushups

Level 2 is where things get real. You graduate from regressions to full-range movements. Diamond pushups shift the load to your triceps. Negative pullups (jump up, lower yourself in 5 seconds) build the eccentric strength you'll need for full pullups. Pike pushups are your gateway to overhead pressing — they directly prepare your shoulders for handstand push-up work down the road.

The Push/Pull/Legs/Core split means each muscle group gets dedicated attention. Volume increases from 3 to 3–4 sets per exercise, and you're training 4 days a week instead of 3.

Progression Gate: Can perform 3×10 full pushups and 3×8 pullup negatives → unlock Level 3.

🔴 Level 3 — Advanced (Weeks 9–12)

Focus: High-intensity bodyweight mastery and skill acquisition

Schedule: 4 days/week — Upper / Lower split

Volume: 4 sets per exercise, 6–12 reps (15–45s for holds)

Exercises: Archer pushups, pseudo planche pushups, pistol squats (or assisted), L-sit holds, full pullups, handstand holds, dragon flags, explosive movements

Level 3 is the summit. This isn't just about being "fit" — it's about body control. Archer pushups demand unilateral strength. Pseudo planche pushups introduce the forward-lean that builds toward a full planche. Pistol squats challenge single-leg strength, balance, and mobility simultaneously. Dragon flags — popularized by Bruce Lee and made famous by Rocky — are the ultimate test of core control.

The Upper/Lower split lets you focus intensity on fewer movements per session while maintaining frequency. Four sets per exercise at 6–12 reps means serious volume at serious difficulty.

Completion: Master all movements with full range of motion and control. You've achieved bodyweight mastery.

Key Principles Behind the Program

1. Earn Your Progressions

Every level has a hard gate. You don't "feel ready" — you demonstrate readiness. This prevents the number-one mistake in bodyweight training: attempting movements you haven't built the foundation for.

2. Volume Scales, Not Just Difficulty

Progression isn't just about harder exercises. Notice how volume increases level to level: 3 sets → 3–4 sets → 4 sets. Days per week increase: 3 → 4 → 4 (with denser splits). You're not just doing fancier pushups — you're doing more work.

3. Full-Body Competence

Push, pull, legs, and core are all represented at every level. No muscle group gets neglected. By Level 3, you'll have built a physique that's balanced, functional, and visibly athletic.

4. Clear, Trackable Metrics

Every session, every set, every rep is designed to be logged. The program is built into IronLog's template system so you can track progression over time. You'll see the numbers climb — and that's powerfully motivating.
